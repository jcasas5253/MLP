Auto insurance that's customized to fit your needs

In addition to the car insurance required by your state, there are other coverages to consider to help protect your finances in the event of an accident or unexpected mishap. Most of these add-on coverages are affordable, and well worth the peace of mind.

LIABILITY

This type of coverage is required of drivers in nearly all states. It covers property damage and injuries you cause to others if you're in at-fault accident. It does not cover the cost(s) of any damage or injuries to you.

Example:

On your daily commute to work, you look away for one second while trying to lower the air conditioning. You hit the car in front of you and the other driver's neck is hurt from the impact. Plus, their back bumper is damaged. Your liability insurance pays for their related medical care and car repairs up to your policy's limits.

COMPREHENSIVE

While not required by state law, comprehensive coverage is recommended for most drivers and is often required by your lender if you are leasing or financing your vehicle. It covers non-collision damage caused to your car.

Example:

Your car is stolen or damaged by a non-collision event (fire, hail, falling objects, vandalism, or flood) and your comprehensive coverage pays for the repairs.

COLLISION

Often purchased with comprehensive coverage, collision protection covers damage to your car in the event of an accident, regardless of who is at fault. This coverage helps pay for repairs or replacement of your vehicle when it's involved in an accident, up to your policy's limits.

Example:

Your front bumper was damaged when you rear-ended the car in front of you on your way to the grocery store. Luckily, your collision coverage pays out for your car's repairs.

UNINSURED/UNDERINSURED

What happens if you're hit by a driver that does not have enough car insurance, or any at all? Uninsured/Underinsured motorist coverage pays for your injuries or damages up to your policy's limits, so that you're not left high and dry.

Example:

You're at a stop sign and a car rear-ends you. The other driver tells you they don't have auto insurance. Your uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage pays for your injured knee and damaged car bumper.

WHY DO I NEED CAR INSURANCE?

Liability insurance is required in nearly every state and is there to protect you financially if you get into a car accident. If that's not reason enough, the additional coverages mentioned above can further cover your injuries and the costs to repair or replace your own vehicle if it gets damaged. Having reliable auto insurance coverage ensures you won't be stuck paying someone else's medical bills or to repair their car out of your own pocket.
